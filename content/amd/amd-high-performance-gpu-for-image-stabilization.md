## The Role of GPUs in Image Stabilization

Graphics Processing Units (GPUs) are specialized processors designed to handle complex mathematical computations, particularly those required for rendering images and videos. Unlike CPUs, which excel at sequential processing, GPUs are optimized for parallel processing, making them ideal for tasks that demand real-time performance. This capability is especially crucial for applications like video editing, gaming, and image stabilization. 

Image stabilization involves correcting distortions caused by motion, vibration, or other external factors. Whether capturing fast-paced sports events or filming scenic landscapes from a moving vehicle, GPUs play a pivotal role in analyzing frames and applying stabilization algorithms at lightning speed.
