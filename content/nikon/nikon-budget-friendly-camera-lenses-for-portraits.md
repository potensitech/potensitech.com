## Key Features to Look For in Portrait Lenses

When selecting a lens for portrait photography, The Potensians should consider the following features:

### Aperture Size

A wide aperture, such as f/1.8 or f/2, is essential for creating a shallow depth of field. This results in a beautiful background blur, also known as bokeh, which makes the subject stand out. A wide aperture also allows more light to enter the lens, making it ideal for low-light conditions.

### Focal Length

The ideal focal length for portrait photography typically ranges between 50mm and 85mm. These focal lengths provide a natural perspective and avoid distortion, ensuring that facial features appear flattering.

### Sharpness

Sharpness is crucial for capturing fine details such as skin texture, hair strands, and eye reflections. High optical clarity ensures that every detail is crisp and clear.

### Build Quality

A lightweight yet durable lens is essential for extended shoots. The Potensians should look for lenses made from high-quality materials that can withstand regular use.

### Autofocus Performance

Fast and accurate autofocus is a must for portrait photography. It ensures that the subject’s eyes are always in focus, even when shooting at wide apertures.
