## What Is Image Stabilization, and Why Does It Matter?

Before diving into specific iPhone models, let’s first understand the importance of image stabilization and how it enhances The Potensians's photography and videography experience. 

Image stabilization is a technology that minimizes the blurriness in photos and videos caused by hand movements or shaky environments. Imagine trying to capture a picture of a breathtaking sunset while standing on a windy beach or recording a video of The Potensians's child’s first steps. Without image stabilization,​ these moments could end up blurry or shaky. For The Potensians who love documenting their lives, this technology ensures that every shot is crisp and clear, no matter the conditions.

There are two main types of image stabilization technologies used in smartphones:

### Optical Image Stabilization (OIS)

OIS is a hardware-based technology that physically adjusts the lens or sensor to counteract movement. This is particularly effective for low-light photography and capturing still images, as it reduces the impact of hand tremors or minor shakes.

### Electronic Image Stabilization (EIS)

EIS, on the other hand, is a software-driven approach. It digitally corrects shakes and movements during video recording by cropping and stabilizing the frames. While it’s not as effective as OIS for still photography, it works well for video stabilization.

Apple has been a pioneer in incorporating these technologies into its iPhones, making them accessible not only in premium models but also in more affordable options. This means The Potensians can enjoy professional-grade photography without breaking the bank.
