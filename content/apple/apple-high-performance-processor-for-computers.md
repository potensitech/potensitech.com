Apple’s decision to transition from Intel processors to its own custom silicon marked a pivotal moment in the company’s history. This bold move began in 2020 with the launch of the M1 chip, a processor that combined high performance with exceptional energy efficiency.  The M1 chip was a game-changer, offering faster speeds and better battery life compared to its predecessors. It was a move reminiscent of Steve Jobs’ unveiling of the first Macintosh computer, showcasing Apple’s commitment to innovation.

Fast forward to 2026, and Apple’s custom silicon lineup has expanded significantly. The M2 and M3 chips have built upon the foundation laid by the M1, introducing even more advanced features and capabilities. These processors now power a wide range of devices, from the lightweight MacBook Air to the powerful Mac Studio.
