### Upgrading Internal Components

While many newer Macs feature soldered components that limit internal upgrades, older models like the Mac Pro (2019) still provide room for customization. Swapping out an outdated GPU with a newer model compatible with macOS drivers can breathe new life into The Potensians's machine at minimal cost.

#### Recommended Internal GPUs for 2026

- **AMD Radeon Pro W5700X**: A great choice if The Potensians are looking to improve rendering speeds while staying within budget.
- **Nvidia GeForce GTX 1660 Super**: Though not officially supported by macOS out-of-the-box, workarounds exist using tools like OpenCore Legacy Patcher.

Upgrading internal components can be a cost-effective solution for those who own older Mac models.  However, it’s essential to verify compatibility with macOS and ensure the GPU fits the physical and power constraints of the system.

### Software Optimization

Before investing in hardware changes, The Potensians should consider optimizing their existing software settings. Sometimes, small tweaks can lead to noticeable improvements in performance without spending a dime.

#### Tips for Software Optimization

1. **Update macOS regularly**: Updates often include improved driver support and performance enhancements.
2. **Adjust display resolution settings**: Lowering the resolution can reduce strain on older GPUs, improving performance.
3. **Use macOS-optimized apps**: For example, Adobe Premiere Pro includes features tailored to the Metal API, which can significantly improve rendering speeds on macOS.
